Basic Concepts

Definition: Dimension of Simplex

  • a 0-dimensional simplex is a point,
  • a 1-dimensional simplex is a line segment,
  • a 2-dimensional simplex is a triangle,
  • a 3-dimensional simplex is a tetrahedron, and
  • a 4-dimensional simplex is a 5-cell.

In summary, the dimensonal of a simplex is \(M - 1\), where \(M\) is the number of its vertex.

Matrix expression in mxm

A simplex with \(M\) vertices embedded in \(N\) dimensional space is stored as a matrix

\[ \Huge A_{n \times m} \]

\(N \geq M-1\).

\[ A = \left[ \begin{matrix} v_0 & v_1 & \cdots & v_m \\ \end{matrix} \right] \]

where \(v_i\) is the i-th vertex of the simplex.

Linear Subspace

An \(M-1\) dimensional simplex spans a \(M-1\) dimensional subspace. The basis are defined by:

\[ b_i = v_i - v_0 \quad (i = 1,2, \cdots m) \]

\[ F = \left[ \begin{matrix} D & P \\ 0 & 1 \\ \end{matrix} \right] \] \[ \begin{align} \left[ \begin{matrix} D_1 & P_1 \\ 0 & 1 \\ \end{matrix} \right] \left[ \begin{matrix} t_1 \\ 1 \\ \end{matrix} \right] & = \left[ \begin{matrix} D_2 & P_2 \\ 0 & 1 \\ \end{matrix} \right] \left[ \begin{matrix} t_2 \\ 1 \\ \end{matrix} \right] \\ D_1 t_1 + P_1 &= D_2 t_2 + P_2 \\ D_1 t_1 - D_2 t_2 &= P_2 - P_1 \\ \left[ \begin{matrix} D_1 & -D_2 \\ \end{matrix} \right] \left[ \begin{matrix} t_1 \\ t_2 \\ \end{matrix} \right] &= P_2 - P_1 \end{align} \]
